We're all in this together

9/19/14

This is my first blog entry and part of a series I will be doing prior to starting at Dev Bootcamp (DBC). For this week we were asked to watch a video on Engineering Empathy and the DBC experience.

After watching the video, Shereef's Fireside Chat, I gained an understanding that the DBC experience is a relationship with owning my education and career that'll last a lifetime. I was moved about the mindset of empowering yourself to take charge of your education, the true passion of ownership. The comparison between being a consumer vs a creator in the video really hit home, as it's so common in our consumer culture that DBC could easily be confused as a product, that, if I just put enough time in money I will get a degree back, whereas my mindset needs to be, own your education and take advantage of this opportunity.

The video inspired me to come into this with that "creator" mindset, not a judgmental one, and take ownership of my education. I may have been guilty of this consumer line of thinking, however after watching the video, I realize that my mindset needed to change. My expectations haven't necessarily changed, before applying, I did my research and knew what I was getting into however the video reinforced my understanding and the need for that right mindset moving forward.

In addition to myself, I'm confident that at DevBootcamp the students and teachers will all have a positive growth mindset that will be encouraging. I'm excited to participate in person and I realize that I need to be aware of common traps when learning at a rapid pace such as "imposter syndrome."

Some other take aways that I learned from the video are the constant evolution of the class itself and it's growth mindset. The constant feedback goes both ways and I appreciate that the class demonstrates what they preach, it makes the experience more authentic and easier to buy into.

Overall, I'm excited about going into DBC with my cohort and taking our education and career into our own hands
